Temperature Controlled Oscillating Fan

November 2021 - December 2021

I used a thermistor to read the current air temperature. I then mounted the fan on top of the servo motor - if the air temperature is greater than 75 degrees F then the arduino will give HIGH voltage to the servo motor and fan creating the oscillation. Once the temperature is below 60 degrees F, both the servo motor and fan are shut off. The current air temperature is programmed to be displayed on the Liquid Crystal Display.

On a small scale, this project was a replica of how tasks can be automated, and gave me a much richer understanding of how eletronics are designed and maintained.
